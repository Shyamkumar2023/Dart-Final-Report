2) Problem Statement

"7 Years. 26 Meters. 1% Margin for Error."

For the last seven years, NUS Calibur Robotics has attempted to engineer a projectile launcher from scratch. The objective is deceptively simple but mechanically grueling: launch custom-designed projectiles to strike a moving target at a distance of 26 meters with absolute accuracy.

Manual reloading and a lack of automated pitch/yaw correction have historically been the primary bottlenecks in student-led ballistic research. Current industry designs often rely on flywheels, springs, or compressed air—methods that introduce significant variance and mechanical fatigue over time.

The Challenge

D.A.R.T. addresses the critical need for a fully closed-loop, self-reloading platform capable of hitting sub-150mm targets while adhering to the rigorous competition rules set by DJI Robomasters.

Overcoming external disturbances, EMF interference, and the complex aerodynamics of zonal-material projectiles required a novice mechatronic approach. This report outlines how we transitioned from theoretical stability to a functional, mission-ready prototype.
