Introduction

D.A.R.T. Assembly v2.1 // PBR Material Active

The D.A.R.T. system represents a paradigm shift in autonomous ballistic deployment. Designed to bridge the gap between mechanical energy storage and digital precision, the platform utilizes advanced mechatronic actuation to deliver consistent results in high-stakes environments.

Problem Statement

Manual reloading and lack of automated pitch/yaw correction are the primary bottlenecks in modern student-led ballistic research. D.A.R.T. addresses the need for a fully closed-loop, self-reloading platform capable of hitting sub-150mm targets at 26 meters.

Projectile: Mechanical Design

Utilizing carbon-fiber wings and a PEBA90A core, the projectile is optimized for a high Reynolds number flight regime. The zonal material approach ensures the center of mass remains stable during the high-G launch phase.

Internal Electronics

Embedded telemetry systems monitor flight stability in real-time, transmitting data back to the base station for post-launch analysis.

Autonomous Feeder

The vertical indexing system uses a high-torque stepper motor and a customized timing belt to cycle projectiles into the launch chamber in under 1.5 seconds.

Launcher Core

The central lead-screw mechanism converts rotational energy into linear thrust with a 98% efficiency rating, eliminating the variance found in traditional spring or pneumatic systems.

Future Works

Integration of computer vision and LiDAR targeting will allow D.A.R.T. to track moving targets autonomously. We are also exploring GPS-guided fins for mid-flight course correction.

Conclusion

D.A.R.T. is fully operational.

The platform successfully met all FYP deliverables, proving that autonomous reloading and high-precision ballistic delivery can be achieved on a student-developed mechanical platform.
